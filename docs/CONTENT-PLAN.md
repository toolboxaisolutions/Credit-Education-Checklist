## UX Copywriting Principles

### Tone
- **Encouraging** — never judgmental
- **Clear** — no financial jargon without explanation
- **Actionable** — every word pushes toward the next step
- **Honest** — no false promises
- **Like a friend** who happens to know credit inside and out

### Language Rules
- ✅ "You've got this" > "Good effort"
- ✅ "Here's exactly what to do" > "Consider doing"
- ✅ "This will take about 10 minutes" > "Complete at your convenience"
- ✅ "Your score moved up 15 points!" > "Credit improvement detected"
- ❌ Never: "You should have..." or "If only you had..."
- ❌ Never: Financial jargon without immediate plain-English translation

## Task Detail Copy (Samples)

### Task 1: Pull Your Free Credit Reports

**Title:** "Pull Your Free Credit Reports"

**Time estimate:** ⏱️ 15 minutes

**Description:**
"Your credit report is like your credit report card — it shows everything lenders see. You need to pull yours from all three bureaus to spot errors, understand where you stand, and know exactly what to fix."

**Why This Matters:**
```
📌 Did you know?
80% of credit reports contain at least one error.
Your first mission: find those errors so we can delete them.
This is the single most impactful thing you can do.
```

**What To Do:**

#### Step 1: Go to AnnualCreditReport.com
This is the ONLY federally authorized source for free credit reports.
⚠️ Watch out for impostor sites — it's ALWAYS annualcreditreport.com

[Link: Go to AnnualCreditReport.com →]

#### Step 2: Pull from All Three Bureaus
- Experian
- Equifax
- TransUnion

Each bureau may have slightly different information. You need all three.

#### Step 3: Download or Print Each Report
You'll want to reference these later.

#### Step 4: Highlight These Sections
When you look at your report, pay attention to:
- ❌ **Accounts you don't recognize** (possible fraud or mixed file)
- ❌ **Late payments that are actually on time** (errors happen)
- ❌ **Accounts still showing balances after payoff**
- ❌ **Collection accounts** from creditors you've already resolved
- ❌ **Old negative items** (most fall off after 7 years)

**Completion Checklist:**
- [ ] Pulled Experian report
- [ ] Pulled Equifax report
- [ ] Pulled TransUnion report
- [ ] Saved/downloaded all three
- [ ] Highlighted any suspicious items

**[Mark Complete →]**

*After completion:*
🎉 "First task done! You now know exactly where you stand. Most people never even look — you're already ahead. Ready for the next step?"

### Task 5: Dispute Inaccuracies

**Title:** "Dispute the Errors on Your Report"

**Time estimate:** ⏱️ 30-45 minutes per bureau

**Why This Matters:**
```
⚡ Quick stat:
A 2012 FTC study found that 1 in 5 consumers had an error
on at least one of their credit reports.

Disputing errors is FREE, legal, and one of the fastest
ways to boost your score.
```

**What To Do:**

#### Step 1: Gather Your Evidence
For each error, you need:
- Copy of the credit report (circle the error)
- Supporting documents (statements, letters, payment records)
- A brief explanation of why it's wrong

#### Step 2: File Disputes with Each Bureau

**Experian:**
Online: experian.com/disputes
Mail: Experian, P.O. Box 4500, Allen, TX 75013
Phone: 1-866-200-6020

**Equifax:**
Online: equifax.com/personal/credit-report
Mail: Equifax, P.O. Box 740256, Atlanta, GA 30374
Phone: 1-866-349-5191

**TransUnion:**
Online: transunion.com/credit-dispute
Mail: TransUnion, P.O. Box 2000, Chester, PA 19016
Phone: 1-800-916-8800

#### Step 3: Use Our Dispute Letter Template
[Download template →]

#### Step 4: Track Your Disputes
Bureaus have 30 days to investigate. Track when you filed and follow up.

**Pro tip:** File online when possible — you can upload documents instantly and track status in real time.

**Completion Checklist:**
- [ ] Gathered evidence for each error
- [ ] Filed dispute with Experian
- [ ] Filed dispute with Equifax
- [ ] Filed dispute with TransUnion
- [ ] Saved confirmation numbers
- [ ] Set reminder to check back in 30 days
