# Marketing Plan — Customer Acquisition Strategy

## Target Market

### Primary Audience
- **Age:** 25-45
- **Credit Score:** 300-629 (poor to fair)
- **Income:** $30k-$80k (can afford $22/mo)
- **Pain Points:** Embarrassed about credit, don't know where to start, overwhelmed by advice

### Secondary Audience
- Recent immigrants (ITIN holders, no credit history)
- Divorced individuals rebuilding credit
- Young adults (18-24) with thin credit files
- People denied for loans/apartments due to credit

### Geographic Focus
- US only (credit system is US-specific)
- Initial focus: California, Texas, Florida, New York (largest populations)

## Customer Acquisition Channels

### 1. Paid Advertising (Primary - Month 1-3)

#### Facebook/Instagram Ads
**Why:** Visual platform, emotional storytelling works, retargeting capabilities

**Ad Creative:**
- **Video 1 (Problem):** Person looking stressed at credit report, text: "Credit score below 600? You're not alone. 45M Americans are in the same boat."
- **Video 2 (Solution):** Screen recording of app walkthrough: "Here's exactly what to do — step by step."
- **Video 3 (Social Proof):** Testimonials from beta users showing score improvements

**Targeting:**
- Interest: Credit repair, financial planning, debt consolidation
- Life events: Recently moved, newly single, recent graduates
- Lookalike audiences from early users

**Budget:** $50-100/day → Scale to $500/day when profitable

**Target Metrics:**
- CAC: $50-80
- CPA (cost per acquisition): $60
- ROAS: 3-5x

#### Google Ads
**Why:** High intent search traffic

**Keywords:**
- "credit repair help" (high volume)
- "how to fix bad credit" (informational)
- "credit repair checklist" (specific intent)
- "credit score 500 what to do" (pain point)
- "best credit cards for bad credit" (commercial intent)

**Ad Copy:**
- "Bad Credit? Here's Your Step-by-Step Fix"
- "45M Americans Have Poor Credit. You Don't Have to Be One."
- "The Credit Repair Checklist. No Guesswork. Just Results."

**Budget:** $20-40/day initially

**Target Metrics:**
- CPC: $2-5 (financial keywords are expensive)
- Conversion rate: 5-8%
- CAC: $60-100

### 2. Content Marketing (Month 2+)

#### Blog/SEO Strategy
**Goal:** Rank for informational credit repair keywords → capture organic traffic

**Content Pillars:**
1. **Credit Repair Basics** (for beginners)
   - "What Is Credit Repair?"
   - "How Long Does It Take to Fix Bad Credit?"
   - "Credit Repair vs. Debt Consolidation: What's Better?"

2. **Disputing Errors** (high-intent)
   - "How to Dispute Credit Report Errors [Step-by-Step]"
   - "Credit Dispute Letter Templates (Free Download)"
   - "What to Do If Your Dispute Is Denied"

3. **Building Credit** (actionable)
   - "Best Credit Cards for Bad Credit (2026)"
   - "How to Build Credit from Scratch"
   - "Secured vs. Unsecured Credit Cards: What's the Difference?"

4. **Score Optimization** (advanced)
   - "How to Improve Credit Score Fast"
   - "Credit Utilization: The Complete Guide"
   - "When to Ask for a Credit Limit Increase"

**Posting Frequency:** 2-3 posts/week (500-1000 words each)

**SEO Tools:** Ahrefs or SEMrush ($100/mo)

**Target Keywords (Year 1):** 100+ keywords ranking page 1

#### YouTube/Videos
**Why:** Credit repair content performs well on YouTube (visual, educational)

**Video Ideas:**
- "I Fixed My Credit Score from 500 to 700 in 12 Months (Here's How)"
- "5 Credit Repair Mistakes That Keep You Poor"
- "How to Read Your Credit Report (Step-by-Step)"
- "Best Credit Cards for Bad Credit [2026]"

**Frequency:** 1 video/week (10-15 min each)

**Monetization:** Ad revenue + affiliate links in description

### 3. Partnerships (Month 3+)

#### Financial Influencers
**Strategy:** Partner with personal finance creators for sponsored content

**Target Creators:**
- The Financial Diet (1M+ subs)
- Graham Stephan (4M+ subs)
- Andre Jikh (2M+ subs)
- Tiffany Aliche (The Budgetnista)

**Deal Structure:**
- $500-2000 per sponsored video/post
- + Affiliate commission (20% recurring)
- + Free Pro subscription

**Tracking:** Custom discount codes, affiliate links

#### Credit Unions & Community Banks
**Why:** They already serve people with credit challenges

**Partnership Model:**
- Offer white-label version of platform
- Credit union refers members → you provide service
- Revenue share: 70/30 split

**Target:** 50 credit unions in Year 1

#### Non-Profit Credit Counseling Agencies
**Why:** They help people with credit problems but lack tech solutions

**Partnership Model:**
- Free or discounted access for their clients
- They provide counselors, you provide platform
- Joint marketing

### 4. Referral Program (Month 2+)

**Mechanism:**
- Current user refers friend → Friend gets 1 month free
- User gets $5 credit for each referral
- Unlimited referrals

**Why:** Word-of-mouth is powerful for financial products

**Target:** 20% of new users come from referrals by Month 6

### 5. Email Marketing (Ongoing)

**Segmentation:**
- New signups (welcome sequence)
- Active users (engagement emails)
- Inactive users (win-back campaign)
- Churned users (reactivation offers)

**Email Types:**
1. Welcome (Day 0, 2, 7, 14)
2. Progress updates (weekly)
3. Task reminders (if inactive 3+ days)
4. Score milestones (congratulations)
5. Card recommendations (when ready)
6. Win-back (if inactive 14+ days)
7. Churn prevention (if cancelling)

**Tools:** SendGrid, ConvertKit, or Mailchimp

**Budget:** $0.01-0.03 per email

## Marketing Funnel

```
Awareness (Ads, Content, Social)
    ↓ (100,000 impressions)
Traffic (Landing Page)
    ↓ (2-5% conversion)
Signup (Free Trial)
    ↓ (30% conversion)
Activation (Complete first task)
    ↓ (60% conversion)
Engagement (Complete 5+ tasks)
    ↓ (50% conversion)
Paid Subscriber ($22/mo)
    ↓ (70% retention)
Retention (6+ months active)
```

**Key Metrics to Track:**
- Impressions → Traffic conversion rate
- Traffic → Signup conversion rate (target: 3-5%)
- Signup → Activation rate (target: 60%)
- Free → Paid conversion rate (target: 20-30%)
- Monthly churn rate (target: <5%)
- LTV:CAC ratio (target: 5:1+)

## Brand Positioning

### Tagline Options:
1. "Your credit didn't break overnight. Fix it step by step."
2. "The roadmap back to good credit."
3. "One step at a time. Your credit, your comeback."
4. "Stop guessing. Start rebuilding."

**Recommendation:** Tagline #1 (most emotional, addresses pain point)

### Brand Voice:
- Encouraging, not preachy
- Clear, not confusing
- Actionable, not theoretical
- Honest, not salesy

### Differentiation:
vs. Credit Karma: "We don't just show you your score. We tell you exactly what to do about it."

vs. Credit Repair Services: "Stop paying $200/month for someone to fix your credit. Do it yourself for $22/month with expert guidance."

vs. DIY Blog Posts: "Blog posts give you information. We give you a roadmap — with checkboxes."

## Launch Strategy

### Pre-Launch (Month -2 to 0)
1. Build landing page with waitlist
2. Start TikTok/Instagram (build audience before product)
3. Write 10 blog posts (SEO head start)
4. Reach out to 50 beta testers
5. Collect testimonials during beta

### Launch Week (Month 0)
1. Product Hunt launch
2. Press outreach (10 publications)
3. Email blast to waitlist
4. Influencer posts (3-5 partners)
5. Facebook/Google ads go live
6. Founder posts on LinkedIn/Twitter

### Post-Launch (Month 1-6)
1. Double down on what works (track CAC per channel)
2. Build referral program
3. Expand content marketing
4. A/B test landing pages
5. Iterate on product based on user feedback

- Monthly new users (target: 100-150)

### Activation
- % who complete first task within 48h (target: 60%)
- % who complete Phase 1 within 14 days (target: 40%)

### Retention
- Monthly churn rate (target: <5%)
- Average subscription length (target: 8+ months)
- % who complete full checklist (target: 20%)

### Revenue
- MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
- LTV (Lifetime Value)
- LTV:CAC ratio (target: 5:1)

### Credit Score Impact
- Average score improvement (target: +100 points in 12 months)
- % who reach 640+ (target: 70% of active users)
- % who reach 700+ (target: 40% of active users)

## Competitive Advantages

1. **Guided experience** — we tell you exactly what to do, not just "here's information"
2. **Visual progress** — node-based map makes progress tangible
3. **Gamification** — badges, streaks, milestones keep you engaged
4. **Affordability** — $22/mo vs. $100-200/mo for services
5. **Personalization** — lifestyle-based card recommendations
6. **Credit report integration** — show score trajectory (Pro tier)

## Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: Credit repair is a crowded market
**Mitigation:** Differentiate on UX (visual, guided) + affordability

### Risk 2: Users don't complete tasks (churn)
**Mitigation:** Strong email/SMS engagement, gamification, community

### Risk 3: Credit bureaus change APIs or pricing
**Mitigation:** Multiple integration options, manual entry fallback

### Risk 4: Regulatory risk (CROA, FCRA)
**Mitigation:** Position as "education" not "repair", consult attorney

### Risk 5: Affiliate programs change terms
**Mitigation:** Multiple card partners, don't rely on single network
